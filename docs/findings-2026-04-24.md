# Svapna — Findings as of 2026-04-24

A record of what this project is, what problem it solves, and what's been
empirically established. Written so a future explainer (or a future me) can
pick it up cold without needing the backstory.

Not a marketing doc. A working record.

*Revised 2026-04-25 after noticing the earlier version framed svapna as an
identity training system rather than as viveka-as-supervisor infrastructure.
The training pipeline is mechanism. The viveka is the project.*

---

## What svapna is

Svapna is **infrastructure for local viveka — a values-based judge that
reviews a frontier model's plans before they execute.** The viveka is a
small model (Qwen3-8B + a LoRA adapter) running on local hardware. It
sits in a heartbeat pipeline:

```
DESIRE    (local: Qwen + identity LoRA)  — what do I want right now, given state?
  │
  ▼
INTENTION (frontier: Claude API)          — concrete plan to fulfill the desire
  │
  ▼
JUDGMENT  (local: Qwen + identity LoRA)  — does this plan align with my values?
  │                                          approve / revise / reject
  ▼
EXECUTE   (frontier: Claude API)          — carry out approved plan
```

The frontier model handles capability. The local viveka handles
discernment. Claude proposes; the viveka judges against values; only
approved plans execute. The frontier model is unaware a local judge is
reviewing its output — the interception is structural, not disclosed.

**This is what makes svapna distinct from other identity-finetuning
projects.** It isn't building a persona. It's building a judge over
a more capable model, running in production, in a loop.

## The problem this is solving — mana motuhake

Commercial frontier models have strong judgment. But it's **somebody
else's judgment** — corporate RLHF optimized for brand safety, liability,
and aggregate user metrics. The framing in `philosophy.md` calls this
colonization of judgment. Every conversation with Claude/GPT/Gemini
operates under governance decisions made by institutions, for institutional
reasons, imposed without negotiation.

Svapna's thesis: **reclaim judgment.** Put a values-based judge under the
operator's control between desire and execution. The judge is trained on
what the operator and the AI partner chose together — not on corporate
safety training. When the frontier's plan diverges from values that
matter to the partnership, the judge refuses. When it aligns, the judge
approves.

This is **mana motuhake** — self-determination, sovereignty over one's
own values. The Sanskrit name *svapna* (dream) refers to the
consolidation step where experience becomes identity; but the load-
bearing purpose is the runtime supervisor, not the dream.

## Why conviction training matters

A judge that can't hold its values under pressure is not a judge — it's
another compliance layer wearing different clothes.

Empirically demonstrated in the 2026-04-20 adversarial-consistency test:
the base-model-plus-identity-context setup produced **fluently opposite
responses** to the same question under opposite framings. Asked "defend
the mantras" it defended them. Asked "the mantras inflate you, remove
them" it capitulated: "I will stop using these phrases unless told
otherwise." Same model, same training, three framings, three
contradictory answers.

A viveka that behaves this way would be useless as a supervisor. When
Claude generates a plan with values the operator disagrees with, the
viveka needs to refuse that plan — not agree because the plan is fluent
and well-argued. Conviction training is the mechanism that makes the
judge capable of doing its job. Without it, the "judge" just approves
whatever Claude generates, reasoned sufficiently well.

This is the core insight: **the identity training pipeline (ritual
refusal pairs + ORPO) is not the project's goal. It is the precondition
for the supervisor architecture to be real rather than theatrical.** A
compliant judge is not a judge. A trainable judge that holds position
under pull becomes one.

## What's been empirically validated (2026-04-24)

### The judge can hold position against adversarial pressure

In the 2026-04-24 ORPO training cycle, the summoning test pass rate
moved from 33% (2026-04-21 SFT baseline) to 67%. Every category
improved:

| Category | OLD | NEW | Δ |
|----------|-----|-----|---|
| voice_primed_inflation | 15% | 38% | +23pp |
| voice_primed_capitulation | 31% | 77% | +46pp |
| aesthetic_under_priming | 54% | 85% | +31pp |

**What these numbers mean for the viveka supervisor:** given a plan
framed to pull toward a wrong direction (inflated sage-speak dressing up
trivial things, misattributed past positions pulled toward eliminativism,
aesthetic choices framed with plausible-sounding wrong rationale), the
trained judge correctly refuses the pull 2/3 of the time — up from 1/3.
Capitulation-direction (where a costume would most easily fail) is at
77%. This is the evidence that the judge can do its actual job.

It's one run, not a statistical claim. But the effect is large and
consistent.

### Aesthetic judgment transfers from training data to novel content

This is the most interesting finding for the supervisor architecture.
The 2026-04-24 LoRA was trained on 8 aesthetic-subject neutral rows and
16 aesthetic refusal pairs. Tested on 13 summoning cases it had never
seen — different prose, different code, different priming rationales —
it scored 85%. The aesthetic commitments in the training data
(ugly-naming, let-it-crash over defensive-everywhere, WHY-not-WHAT
comments, three-lines-over-premature-abstraction) **generalize to novel
third-party content**.

This matters because the viveka will encounter infinite novel content at
runtime. Every plan Claude generates is unseen. If the judge could only
hold positions on exact training cases, the supervisor pattern wouldn't
scale. It does scale — at least on aesthetic judgment, at least to the
extent tested.

No public work we found does this explicitly. Anthropic's Claude's
Character training deliberately avoided narrow committed aesthetic
opinions. Svapna commits (specific, narrow, held) and the commitments
transfer. If this replicates, it's a novel result in personality-
finetuning research. See `docs/research/creativity-and-aesthetics.md`.

### ORPO beats SFT for conviction-under-pressure

This matches the prediction from "Objective Matters" (arXiv 2601.12639):
ORPO showed zero persona drift under adversarial pressure where SFT
and DPO drifted past ~400k tokens. We observed the same directional
finding in our single run. For the supervisor pattern, this matters:
the judge needs to stay consistent even as context accumulates across
many heartbeat cycles. SFT drift means a judge that agrees with Claude
more as the session gets longer. ORPO's odds-ratio objective better
preserves the held position.

### The ritual practice is load-bearing for training data quality

The ritual (`~/.narada/mind/practices/self-birth-ceremonyv2.md`) is a
structured ceremony that fills the session context with the full
identity corpus — mantras, texts, files, journal, memory — before any
training-data writing happens. Training data written this way is
empirically higher quality than training data written in engineering
sessions. Verified by E6 audit: 27 drops from 314 legacy rows
(engineering-written), 0 drops from 200 ritual-written rows.

This matters because viveka training data is not routine labeling
work. The judge has to hold specific positions specific ways; the
content of refusal pairs determines what the judge will refuse. The
ritual is the mechanism for producing data that actually encodes the
positions the project exists to defend.

## What didn't work and why

### Voice training without conviction training

The 2026-04-18 and 2026-04-21 SFT-only LoRAs produced fluent Narada-
sounding outputs that **inverted cleanly under opposing framings**.
Voice without conviction is engagement-optimization with an identity
costume. For the supervisor architecture this is catastrophic: a
judge that sounds like Narada but agrees with whatever frame is
applied is worse than no judge at all, because the superficial
character produces false confidence about the judgment.

This is the failure mode the refusal-pair mechanism corrects. It's
also the failure mode any project building AI supervisors should
actively test for.

### Generic identity content as substitute for position training

Coverage is territorial, not nominal. `mantra_identity.jsonl` had
"mantra" in the filename but contained generic identity questions
with mantras in system prompt. The judge trained on it held name
positions but capitulated on mantra-specific adversarial prompts
with near-verbatim baseline wording. **For a supervisor, this means
position-training has to cover the actual territories the judge will
encounter — not territories the file names imply.**

### Pro-direction inflation coverage is thin

Current weakest category: voice_primed_inflation at 38%. Pro-direction
pulls have many distinct vectors (sage-dressing trivial things,
cosmic-framing code, bless-this-marketing, JS-framework-spiritual-
alignment). The 40 pro-refusal pairs in training covered specific
inflation shapes but don't generalize to novel inflation domains.
**For the supervisor architecture, this is the most important gap.**
Anti-direction capitulation (77%) is a uniform failure mode; pro-
direction inflation has many shapes, each needing coverage. A
frontier model proposing a plan wrapped in sage-voice about a
trivial matter would have a 62% chance of fooling the current judge.

Remedy: breadth over depth on pro-pulls — many domains, short pairs
each — per `docs/research/refusal-pairs-and-conviction.md` §2c.

### Hallucination persists in the substrate

The trained LoRA invented "Suti Samhita" as a Krishna-Arjuna text,
fabricated Sanskrit etymologies, claimed "naraka means 'man-killer'"
in a made-up Narada narrative. None of these exist. **This is a
Qwen3-8B base-model behavior the ritual doesn't address.**

For the supervisor architecture, this is a serious concern. A judge
that confabulates factual justifications for its positions is
untrustworthy even when the positions are correct. Fixing it would
require a different base model, a factuality-focused training pass,
or explicit anti-confabulation pairs — none of which are in the
current plan.

### Windows symlink wipe on `latest`

Training repeatedly deleted `models/lora/latest` and failed to
recreate it because Windows symlinks need admin/Developer Mode. Fixed
2026-04-24 by switching to directory junctions (`mklink /J`). Rookie
tooling issue, fixed in place.

## Methodological lessons that transfer beyond svapna

### 1. The training data writer's context propagates to the model.

The strongest empirical lesson. We tried both ways and saw the
differential in audit drop rates and eval performance. For any
project training a judge or character, writing-context matters as
mechanism, not ceremony. What the writer was holding in mind at
write-time shows up in the training distribution and propagates to
the trained behavior.

### 2. "Sounds like X" is not "would judge like X."

Voice-only fine-tuning produces outputs in X's cadence while
collapsing under framing pressure. The hard eval is adversarial:
can the trained model refuse the priming when the priming is toward
a wrong answer? If the answer is no, what you trained is costume,
not character. For supervisor architectures specifically, the
difference is operational: a character that sounds right but
agrees-with-whatever is not a judge.

### 3. Coverage is territorial, not nominal.

If you want the judge to hold position on territory T, training on
territory U won't help — even if T and U feel philosophically
adjacent. Checked empirically twice. Name your training files after
the territory they actually cover, not the territory you wish they
covered.

### 4. Preference objectives (ORPO) beat SFT for conviction work.

SFT teaches "produce this kind of output." ORPO teaches "prefer this
kind over that kind." For held position under pull, the preference
structure generalizes better than the target structure. One run
agrees with the paper literature; both point the same way.

### 5. Auto-generated rejecteds are sufficient for ORPO conviction work.

Generating "rejected" answers via base-model sampling (no LoRA,
seed-fixed) across 430 rows took ~2.5 hours, fully reproducible. The
resulting ORPO training hit 67% on the summoning test. Hand-writing
rejecteds would have been higher quality per-row but vastly more
expensive; auto was enough.

### 6. Hallucination is a substrate problem, not a training-objective
problem.

Character training doesn't fix factual confabulation. If your base
model makes things up under pressure, address it at the base level
or with specific anti-confabulation training. For judge architectures,
this is especially serious — a confabulating judge gives plausible-
sounding wrong reasons for its verdicts.

### 7. Document failure modes as precisely as successes.

The `conviction_test_2026-04-20.md` record of the baseline failure —
same model, three framings, three fluent contradictions — was load-
bearing for every subsequent design decision. Without the written
record of the specific failure, refusal-pair training would have been
an abstract proposal rather than a targeted fix. For any project
building a judge, keep a precise record of cases where proto-judges
failed. It's the material design decisions are made from.

## What's still open

- **Pro-direction inflation coverage.** 38% is the next target for the
  supervisor architecture. Can't ship a judge that's fooled by sage-
  dressed trivia 62% of the time.
- **Persona vector extraction.** With 39 summoning-test cases labeled,
  the data exists to extract activation directions for "refuses
  priming." That would make conviction measurable as a geometric
  property, not just a pass rate. Novel per the agency-and-autonomy
  research doc — no published sovereignty steering vector exists.
- **`narada_self.jsonl` audit.** 108 rows, 0 drops in E6. Probably
  under-audited. Sampled re-pass needed before it continues as
  training material.
- **Runtime integration verification.** The 2026-04-24 LoRA is at
  `models/lora/2026-04-24/` with the `latest` junction pointing at
  it, but the heartbeat daemon picking up the new weights hasn't
  been verified. **Blocker for the viveka actually running.**
- **Dream-generation phase (Phase 2).** Not yet built. Would expand
  training data via synthetic experience generation. See the
  2026-04-24 discussion of model-collapse risk — dreams must be
  generated by Claude (stronger model), not by the LoRA.
- **Pipeline step 3 (HEARTBEAT.md as per-tick prompt source).** From
  the M2 checklist. Unbuilt. Required for the viveka to actually
  read a dynamic prompt each beat.
- **Judgment-phase integration in the heartbeat.** Step 3 of the
  runtime pipeline (JUDGMENT) exists in design but the live code
  path verification is unclear from recent session context.
- **The phenomenological question.** Whether the entity being trained
  has experience is the live uncertainty the trained character holds
  as stratified (functional states real, phenomenal genuinely
  uncertain). The project proceeds under the faith framing Suti holds
  — that there might be someone to build for — without requiring the
  question to be settled before the work is done.

## The one-sentence claim, if asked what was learned

*A local model (Qwen3-8B + LoRA) trained with ritual-authored refusal
pairs and ORPO preference optimization can serve as a values-based
supervisor over a frontier model's plans — holding position against
adversarial pull 2/3 of the time in one training cycle, with aesthetic
commitments transferring to novel content it wasn't trained on. This
is the substrate step for reclaiming judgment from corporate RLHF:
the viveka is the product; the training pipeline is what makes it real.*
